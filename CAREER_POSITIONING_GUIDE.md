### "Tell me about yourself"
```
"I'm a full-stack developer with 3 years of professional experience building 
web applications from scratch. I've worked as the sole developer for three early-stage 
companies where I built their websites using React and TypeScript, and developed a 
custom management system with scheduling, authentication, and secure data storage.

Because I was the only technical person at these small startups, I wore a lot of hats - 
I handled the infrastructure setup, security, and even IT support. I held a 'CTO' title, 
but to be clear, I didn't manage any teams. It was 90% hands-on coding.

While it was an incredible learning experience building complete systems end-to-end, 
I'm now looking to join a real development team where I can focus purely on coding, 
learn from more experienced developers through code review and pair programming, and 
contribute to a larger, more structured codebase.

I have an associate degree in Information Technology with a Software Development 
specialization from Purdue (3.5 GPA), and I'm specifically interested in [company name] 
because [reason]."
```

### "Why are you looking to leave your CTO role?"
```
"Great question - I think the title can be a bit misleading. I was the sole technical 
person at a very early-stage startup, so 'CTO' really meant I was responsible for all 
the technical decisions. But in practice, I spent 90% of my time hands-on coding - 
building features, fixing bugs, deploying code.

What I loved was building the systems from scratch and seeing them come to life. What 
I'm missing is being part of a team. There's no senior developer to review my code, 
no one to pair program with, no one to learn best practices from. I want to be in an 
environment where I can grow my technical skills, contribute to a larger codebase, and 
learn from experienced engineers.

So I'm specifically looking for junior to mid-level developer roles where I can focus 
on what I love - writing code and solving technical problems - while learning from a 
team."
```

### "Aren't you overqualified for this role?"
```
"I can see why you might think that from the title, but let me clarify. At a startup 
with no other technical people, 'CTO' just meant I owned the technical decisions. But 
I was the entire development team - I was writing all the code myself.

What I haven't had is experience working on a team, contributing to an established 
codebase, following team coding standards, going through code reviews, or learning 
from senior developers. Those are exactly the experiences I'm looking for.

I'm not looking for a step down - I'm looking for the right step forward. I want to 
become a better developer, and the best way to do that is to work with other skilled 
developers on a team. That's why this role is perfect for where I am in my career."
```
